|---|---|

### Learning Objectives
- Understand the history and purpose of money
- Learn fundamental budgeting and saving skills
- Understand how people earn money (jobs, entrepreneurship)
- Distinguish needs from wants and make smart spending decisions
- Grasp basic investing concepts (compound interest, stocks)
- Understand how banks work
- Appreciate the value of giving and sharing

## 2. Proposed Topics

### Topic 1: What Is Money? üí∞
**Subtitle:** From Trading Cows to Digital Dollars

**Key Concepts:** Barter system, commodity money, coins, paper money, digital currency

**Content Outline:**
1. **Before Money: The Barter System** ‚Äî Trading goods directly (a chicken for some wheat). Problems: what if no one wants your chicken?
2. **First Forms of Money** ‚Äî Shells, salt, tea bricks, and other commodity money across cultures
3. **Coins Are Born** ‚Äî The Lydians (modern Turkey) created the first standardized coins ~600 BCE
4. **Paper Money Takes Over** ‚Äî China invented paper money during the Tang Dynasty (~700 CE)
5. **Modern Money** ‚Äî Credit cards, digital payments, cryptocurrency basics
6. **What Gives Money Value?** ‚Äî Trust, government backing, scarcity

**Characters:**
| Emoji | Name | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| üêÑ | Barterin' Betty | The Trader | Tries to trade her cow for everything she needs ‚Äî and learns why money was invented! |
| ü™ô | Coinsworth | The Coin Collector | A wise old coin who has traveled the world and seen how different cultures use money |
| üí≥ | Digi | The Digital Dollar | A modern digital payment who explains how money works in the 21st century |

**Fun Facts:**
- The word "salary" comes from the Latin word "salarium" ‚Äî Roman soldiers were sometimes paid in salt!
- The world's largest coin is Australian ‚Äî it weighs over 1 tonne and is worth $1 million AUD
- About 92% of the world's money exists only digitally ‚Äî not as physical cash
- The first credit card was invented in 1950 after a businessman forgot his wallet at dinner

**Quiz Questions (5 sample):**
1. What was the biggest problem with the barter system? ‚Üí You needed to find someone who wanted exactly what you had ‚úÖ
2. Which civilization created the first standardized coins? ‚Üí The Lydians ‚úÖ
3. Where was paper money invented? ‚Üí China ‚úÖ
4. What does the word "salary" originally relate to? ‚Üí Salt ‚úÖ
5. What percentage of the world's money exists only digitally? ‚Üí About 92% ‚úÖ

**Essay Prompt:** "If you could invent a brand new type of money, what would it look like? What would it be made of? How would people use it? Explain why your new money would be better than what we use today."

**Reward Game: Barter Town Builder** ‚Äî Trade goods in an ancient village using barter, then commodity money, then coins. Timer shows how each innovation speeds up trade. Players experience the "double coincidence of wants" problem firsthand.

### Topic 2: Saving & Budgeting üê∑
**Subtitle:** Making Your Money Work for You

**Key Concepts:** Saving goals, budgets, delayed gratification, pay yourself first, emergency funds

**Content Outline:**
1. **Why Save?** ‚Äî Short-term goals (toys, games) vs. long-term goals (college, car)
2. **The Three Jars** ‚Äî Save, Spend, Share ‚Äî splitting money into categories
3. **Making a Budget** ‚Äî Tracking what comes in and what goes out
4. **The Marshmallow Test** ‚Äî Famous experiment on delayed gratification
5. **Setting SMART Goals** ‚Äî Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound savings goals
6. **Tips & Tricks** ‚Äî Automatic saving, visual progress trackers, avoiding impulse buys

**Characters:**
| Emoji | Name | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| üê∑ | Penny the Piggy | The Savings Coach | A cheerful piggy bank who teaches kids to set savings goals and watch their money grow |
| üìä | Budgie the Budget Bird | The Planner | Loves organizing money into categories and tracking every penny |
| üç¨ | Temptation Tim | The Impulse Monster | Always tries to get kids to spend NOW ‚Äî but they learn to resist! |

**Fun Facts:**
- Warren Buffett (one of the richest people ever) bought his first stock at age 11!
- The famous "marshmallow experiment" showed that kids who could wait for a second marshmallow often did better in school years later
- In Japan, kids receive special envelopes called "otoshidama" with money for New Year ‚Äî and many are taught to save most of it
- The 50/30/20 rule suggests: 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings

**Reward Game: Piggy Bank Planner** ‚Äî Choose a savings goal, split weekly allowance into Save/Spend/Share jars, resist temptation pop-ups, and fill a visual savings thermometer. Includes random events (birthday money, unexpected expense) that teach adaptability.

### Topic 4: Spending Wisely üõí
**Subtitle:** Needs, Wants, and Smart Choices

**Key Concepts:** Needs vs. wants, comparison shopping, advertising awareness, value for money

**Content Outline:**
1. **Needs vs. Wants** ‚Äî Food is a need; a candy bar is a want
2. **The Advertising Trap** ‚Äî How ads try to make you want things you don't need
3. **Comparison Shopping** ‚Äî Checking prices, reading reviews, finding deals
4. **The 24-Hour Rule** ‚Äî Wait before buying to avoid impulse purchases
5. **Getting the Best Value** ‚Äî Price per unit, quality vs. quantity, sales
6. **Making a Shopping Plan** ‚Äî Lists, budgets, and sticking to them

**Characters:**
| Emoji | Name | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| üõí | Smarty Cart | The Wise Shopper | A shopping cart who helps kids navigate stores and make smart purchase decisions |

**Reward Game: Smart Shopper Challenge** ‚Äî Shop on a budget in a virtual store. Distinguish needs from wants, compare prices across stores, resist flashy advertising pop-ups, and try to maximize value within your budget. Score based on how much of your "needs" list you fulfill while staying under budget.

### Topic 5: Investing Basics üìà
**Subtitle:** Growing Your Money Like a Garden

**Key Concepts:** Investing vs. saving, compound interest, stocks, bonds, risk vs. reward, diversification

**Content Outline:**
1. **What Is Investing?** ‚Äî Putting money to work so it grows over time
2. **The Magic of Compound Interest** ‚Äî Einstein allegedly called it the "eighth wonder of the world"
3. **What Are Stocks?** ‚Äî Owning a tiny piece of a company
4. **What Are Bonds?** ‚Äî Lending money to companies or governments
5. **Risk vs. Reward** ‚Äî Why riskier investments can pay more (or lose more)
6. **Start Early!** ‚Äî The incredible power of time in investing

**Characters:**
| Emoji | Name | Title | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| üå± | Sprout | The Growth Expert | A little seed that shows how small investments grow into mighty money trees over time |
| üìä | Stocky | The Market Watcher | Explains what stocks are by turning kids into "part owners" of their favorite companies |
| ‚öñÔ∏è | Balancer | The Risk Manager | Teaches about putting eggs in different baskets (diversification) |

**Fun Facts:**
- If you invested $1 at 10% annual return, in 50 years it would grow to over $117!
- The New York Stock Exchange started in 1792 under a buttonwood tree on Wall Street
- Warren Buffett made 99% of his wealth AFTER his 50th birthday ‚Äî that's compound interest at work!

**Reward Game: Compound Growth Garden** ‚Äî Plant "money seeds" with different risk levels (safe savings = slow-growing flowers, stocks = faster trees that might wilt, bonds = steady shrubs). Watch them grow over simulated years. Branches grow their own branches to visualize compounding. Goal: grow the most valuable garden.

## 5. Open Questions

1. **Age-appropriate depth:** Should investing/stock market content be simplified for 6-8 year olds or aimed at 9-12?
2. **Real-world currency:** Should we focus on US currency or include examples from multiple countries?
3. **Digital money/crypto:** How much detail on cryptocurrency is appropriate for kids?
4. **Video length:** Many of the best financial literacy videos (Biz Kid$) are ~25 min. Embed full episodes or timestamp segments?
5. **Reward game complexity:** The Bank Manager Simulator is the most complex proposed game ‚Äî should we simplify it?
